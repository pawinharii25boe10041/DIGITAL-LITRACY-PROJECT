# Social Media Etiquette & Responsibility Checklist

Maintaining a professional and positive digital footprint is essential for college students as they prepare for the professional world. Use this checklist to audit your social media habits.

## 5 Do's (Best Practices)

| Action | Why It Matters |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Do Privacy Audits** | Regularly check your settings to ensure only intended audiences see your personal posts. |
| **Do Network Purposefully** | Connect with professors, alumni, and industry professionals on platforms like LinkedIn to build your career. |
| **Do Think Before You Post** | Ask yourself if you would be comfortable with a future employer or a university dean reading your content. |
| **Do Fact-Check Content** | Verify information before sharing to avoid spreading misinformation or "fake news." |
| **Do Showcase Achievements** | Use your platforms to highlight projects, volunteer work, and academic milestones. |

---

## 5 Don'ts (Common Pitfalls)

*   **Don't Badmouth Institutions:** Avoid posting negative comments about your college, professors, or former employers, as this reflects poorly on your professionalism.
*   **Don't Overshare Personal Details:** Keep sensitive information like your home address, student ID number, or current exact location private for safety.
*   **Don't Engage with Trolls:** Avoid getting into heated public arguments or "flame wars." It’s better to disengage or block than to look unprofessional.
*   **Don't Post Illegal Content:** Never post photos or text referencing underage drinking, illicit substances, or any activities that violate university codes of conduct.
*   **Don't Ignore Tags:** Don't let others define your digital footprint; untag yourself from photos or posts that don't align with the image you want to project.
