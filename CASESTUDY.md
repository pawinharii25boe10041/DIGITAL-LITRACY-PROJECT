# Case Study: The "Urgent Payroll" Phishing Attack

### What the Crime Is
Phishing is a form of social engineering where attackers masquerade as a trusted entity—such as a bank, a university, or a popular service provider—to trick victims into revealing sensitive information like login credentials, credit card numbers, or Social Security digits.

### How It Happens (Step-by-Step)
1.  **The Hook:** The attacker sends a spoofed email to a student’s university account. The subject line reads: *"URGENT: Action Required on Your Financial Aid Disbursement."*
2.  **The Deception:** The email uses the university’s official logo and colors. It claims there is a "system error" and the student must log in immediately to confirm their banking details or risk losing their semester refund.
3.  **The Trap:** The victim clicks a button labeled "Verify Account," which directs them to a fraudulent website that looks identical to the university’s actual login portal.
4.  **The Theft:** The student enters their username and password. The attacker captures these credentials in real-time.
5.  **The Payload:** Once the attacker has access, they log into the real student portal to change the direct deposit settings to their own offshore account and harvest personal data for identity theft.

### Who Is Targeted
College students are primary targets because they frequently manage financial aid, use campus-wide digital portals, and are often under high stress, making them more likely to react quickly to "urgent" alerts without verifying the source.

### Consequences
The consequences are severe: students can lose thousands of dollars in tuition refunds, suffer long-term credit damage from identity theft, and lose access to vital academic records. On a larger scale, these attacks can compromise the entire university network if the stolen credentials have administrative privileges.
