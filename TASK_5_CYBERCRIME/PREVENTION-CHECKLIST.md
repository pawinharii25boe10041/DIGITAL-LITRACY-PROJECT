# Stay Safe Online: Prevention Checklist for College Students

This checklist provides actionable steps to protect your digital identity and financial assets while navigating campus life and online services in India.

## General Digital Safety
- [ ] **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Activate 2FA on your university email, social media, and Google/Apple accounts using an authenticator app rather than just SMS.
- [ ] **Audit App Permissions:** Regularly check your smartphone settings to ensure apps don't have unnecessary access to your microphone, camera, or contacts.
- [ ] **Use Strong, Unique Passwords:** Use a password manager to maintain complex passwords; avoid using your birthdate, "123456", or your college roll number.
- [ ] **Beware of Public Wi-Fi:** Avoid logging into sensitive accounts (like student portals or banks) while using free campus or cafe Wi-Fi. Use a VPN if necessary.
- [ ] **Think Before You Click:** Inspect the sender's email address and hover over links to check the actual URL before clicking on "urgent" notices about exams or fees.
- [ ] **Verify "Job Offer" Messages:** Be skeptical of high-paying part-time job offers sent via WhatsApp or Telegram that ask for an initial "processing fee."

## Financial & UPI Safety (India Specific)
- [ ] **Remember: PIN is only for Sending Money:** Never enter your UPI PIN to *receive* money. If someone claims they are sending you a refund or a prize and asks for your PIN, it is a scam.
- [ ] **Verify UPI ID/QR Codes:** Before hitting 'Pay' at a merchant or for a shared bill, double-check the name displayed on the screen to ensure the QR code hasn't been tampered with.

## Reporting Cybercrimes
If you or someone you know falls victim to a cybercrime, immediate reporting is crucial:
1.  **National Helpline:** Dial **1930** immediately to report financial fraud and trigger the "golden hour" protocol to freeze the stolen funds.
2.  **Online Portal:** File a formal complaint at the official **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal**: [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in).
3.  **Local Authority:** You can also visit the nearest **Cyber Cell** at your local police station.
